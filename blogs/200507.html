I've always been a bit of a pessimist and the COVID situation outlook is no different. I hate to be so smug about this but I saw it coming all the way back in January and left the city. I saw a rush on grocery stores and bought plenty of food water, medicine, and toilet paper half way through February and I knew Americans couldnt stay quarantined long enough to actually defeat this thing (as of this week I believe states such as Texas and Georgia are fully open and back to buisiness though I am not sure because I honestly stopped paying much attention, been more focused on my website) but I have come to another, bleak, conclusion: This is the end of society as we know it. The virus is unlike anything we humans have dealt with before and I truly believe it was created in the Wuhan lab (not as a bioweapon obviously!) I am worried about many things A) Why do some healthy people die while some extremely old people live? B) Why are our tests so innacurate? C) Does infection give a temporary or long-term immunity? D) Is it possible the virus stays dormant in your body like Herpes or HIV? Many more things as well but its more of a vibe than a scientific process (which I trust but for the record Elon Musk also advised me "Dont doubt your vibe, it knows it's true")





I am of the opinion that re-opening will only kill more and more people and that the damage done to the economy already is irreversable. I foresee mass poverty, food shortages, violence, death, possibly even war (civil or against China or both) My old job required hundreds of people crowded together each afternoon for lunch. Yeah thats over. So many small buisiness are dead in the water, only to be replaced by the mega-marts of our reality. So many previously free, or at least tangentially free men and women have been reduced to slaves of the system by the virus. I worry that forging your own path, opening a buisiness, or selling grilled cheese in a parking lot will never return and vanishing with it some of my favorite ways of life.
