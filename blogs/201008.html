I'm not sure I'll be able to keep this up (I remember back in May I was in a similarly motivated spirit and March before that) but this fills the gap left by Minecraft, for now.





On the books there's not much to say about Hamlet. Easily one of the greatest pieces of literature of all time. This was actually my first time reading it all the way through in one sitting and it was so nice to imagine the entire thing play out in my mind's eye. Hamlet's interactions with Ophelia are, ahem, based as fuck, and I noticed for the first time that Claudius is not evil incarnate. Or well, there's more to the back story than meets the eye. Hamlet's father was possibly an ineffective king and especially given the anger of Fortinbras, Claudius may have rightly believed it was for the best to assume the throne himself....But to kill his brother in his sleep..IDK it's a bigger play than I have the brain to discuss. It's larger than I am and I am relieved to be able to admit that.





Sartor Resartus was tougher to get through. It reminded me of an obscure red book I must have found in the library last year (but who's name eludes me still) in that it was dry, had too many annotations, and I felt the author was full of himself. There were several, probably a dozen or so, very introspectively wise lines (All must work or steal) or ideas Carlyle presented but I found that it dragged on in certain places a bit too much. The Blumine story was remarkably on the nose and served to remind me how un-special I am. The christian / anti-utilitarian / anti-republican arguments were unpleasant to me and that surely soured my opinion on the book overall but I tried my best to stay impartial and finish it, which I did. I think it made me a better reader but it was somewhat mentally exhausting, it was not like the Hobbit which I can read half stoned and still digest, I had to be nearly stone cold sober to read Sartor Resartus at a decent rate.





I'm going to start my rice in the microwave then I'll be back and discuss the debate.





Okay so today was the Vice Presidential Debate, well yesterday was, but it was only 11 hours ago, and I'm still awake. I watched it live and was pleasantly surprised to hear Robert listening as well in the living room. I believe firmly that anyone who wants to be informed owes it to themselves to watch the primary source itself. Regarding their performance I was expecting Harris to wipe the floor with him. I didn't remember Pence ever seeming charismatic in 2016 and I thought Harris' entire campaign last year was essentially her debate prowess (which I always saw as a scam anyways. There was one democratic debate where I noticed her overtly leaning on rhetorical devices and while proper oration I found it disingenuous which has always been my biggest complaint with the Democratic Party) and her ability to 'take on Donald Trump on the debate stage' or some words similar to those. What I saw was quite the opposite.





The overall impression I got from the two of them was Pence seemed calm, intelligent, and honestly a better choice for the Republican Party while Harris seemed...bitchy. To her credit I do believe Pence interrupted her first but after the 2nd time hearing her 'excuse me I'm speaking' soundbite coupled with her fake smile I had already made up my mind. I suppose I ought to admit I'm biased against Harris and I've despised her since her run for Senate in 2016. So if anyone else is reading this take that into account, this is a biased review and you should go watch the debate for yourself and come to your own conclusions.





The most standout moment for me was when Pence cornered Harris over Court Packing (and of course the fly that landed on his head!) That was one of my biggest complaints about the first debate was Joe!'s refusal to answer the question. To be fair Pence essentially dodged the question on abortion and others as well in a typical political way. Coming away from it I had a much better impression of Pence than I had before. He at the very least made a competent argument for fiscal and social conservatism which I hadn't heard put so elegantly in probably 8 years since the Obama/Romney debates. I found this debate to be MUCH more watchable than the first one and I personally believe they should do all of the debates with the candidates sitting down. I think it makes them calm down and not shout over each other as much.
